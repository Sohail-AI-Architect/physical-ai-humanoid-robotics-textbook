# Introduction to Physical AI

**Weeks 1–2** | Foundations of Embodied Intelligence

This section introduces the core concepts of Physical AI — the study of artificial
intelligence systems that interact with and operate in the physical world through
robotic bodies. Unlike purely software-based AI (chatbots, image classifiers), Physical AI
must perceive, reason about, and act within real, unpredictable environments.

## What Is Physical AI?

Physical AI refers to AI systems embedded in physical agents — robots, autonomous vehicles,
drones — that must:

- **Perceive** the world through sensors (cameras, LiDAR, IMUs, force-torque sensors)
- **Reason** about spatial relationships, physics, and human intent
- **Act** through actuators (motors, grippers, legs) with real-world consequences

The humanoid form factor is particularly interesting because it allows robots to operate in
environments designed for humans — offices, homes, factories — without requiring
infrastructure modifications.
